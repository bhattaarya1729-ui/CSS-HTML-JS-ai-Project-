Gupta Empire - The Golden Age of Ancient India

"The Gupta Empire is regarded as the Golden Age of Ancient India"

Rise of the Gupta Dynasty

Post-Mauryan Period

After the Post-Mauryan Period, the Gupta dynasty emerged in northern India, reunifying the subcontinent under a powerful all-India empire by subjugating regional rulers and laying the foundation of a new imperial order.

Emergence of Regional Dynasties in North India

During this period, three major dynasties emerged across North and Central India: the Guptas in Eastern India, the Nagas in western Madhya Pradesh, and the Vakatakas in the Deccan region.

Status of Early Gupta Rulers

The early Gupta rulers are believed to have been subordinates of the Kushanas, serving as their feudatories (samantas).

Rulers of the Gupta Dynasty (319 AD – 550 AD)

Chandragupta I (319–335 AD)

  • Founder of the Gupta Empire
  • First independent Gupta ruler
  • Capital: Pataliputra
  • Title: Maharajadhiraja
  • Gupta Samvat began in 319–320 AD

Marriage Alliance

  • Married Kumaradevi (Lichchhavi princess)
  • Strengthened political power and expansion
  • Issued Dampatya / Rajdampatya gold coins

Samudragupta (335–380 AD)

  • Son of Chandragupta I and Kumaradevi
  • Greatest conqueror of the Gupta Dynasty
  • Called "Napoleon of India" (V. A. Smith)

Major Titles

  • Lichchhavi Dauhitra
  • Param Bhagavata
  • Kaviraja
  • Sarvarajochchetta
  • Ashvamedha Prakramanka

Prayag Prashasti (Allahabad Pillar Inscription)

  • Main source on Samudragupta
  • Author: Harisena
  • Language: Sanskrit
  • Script: Brahmi
  • Style: Kavya / Champu
  • 33 lines on Samudragupta
  • No dates mentioned

Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya) (380–414 AD)

  • Defeated Shaka ruler Rudrasimha III
  • Titles: Vikramaditya, Shakari, Param Bhagavata

Ramagupta Episode

  • Known only from literary sources
  • Main source: Devichandraguptam

Navratnas (Nine Gems of Chandragupta II's Court)

Kumaragupta I (414–455 AD)

  • Longest reigning Gupta ruler
  • Founded Nalanda University
  • Titles: Mahendraditya, Ashvamedha-Mahendra
  • Last phase of the Gupta Golden Age

Skandagupta (455–467 AD)

  • Last powerful Gupta ruler
  • Successfully resisted Huna invasions
  • Bhitari Pillar Inscription
  • Repaired Sudarshan Lake (Junagadh Inscription)

Decline of the Gupta Dynasty

  • Continuous Huna invasions
  • Weak successors
  • Last known ruler: Vishnugupta
